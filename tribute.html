About Araki

Araki Hirohiko is a famous Japanese mangaka known for his incredibly popular manga / anime series JoJo's Bizarre Adventure. He was born on 7th of June, 1960 in Japan. Araki has contributed to the Manga and Anime industry not only through his popular series, but also acting as an inspiration, which can be seen through other popular works in the form of references made by other Mangakas.

    While you're at it, here are some fun facts about Araki-Sensei:
  1. Araki develops the personality of his characters based on their stand ability, as stated in his book "Manga in Theory and Practice".
  2. Araki drew inspiration from a manga named "The Fist of The North Star" (it is the origin of the "Omae wa, mou shinde iru. Nani!?" meme!)
  3. JoJo currently has more chapters than One Piece, which currently has around 1000+ episodes, while JoJo only has 190 episodes!
  4. Araki's main inspiration came from popular hollywood movies in his childhood, which pretty much explains the use of western culture and music in the JoJo manga and anime.

Mangas Written by Araki

Araki's series, JoJo's Bizarre Adventure is known for it's unique artstyle and the manga has been serialized and published by Shueisha on January 1, 1987. Currently, JoJo is in it's 9th part, known as JOJOLands. Every story has a different protagonist and each protagonist has a different ability, personality, and story to follow them.

Genres: Action, Adventure, Supernatural.
